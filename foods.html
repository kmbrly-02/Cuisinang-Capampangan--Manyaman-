CUISINE

LENGUA

Lengua is a tender, flavorful Kapampangan dish made from ox tongue, often served in a rich, creamy sauce, and enjoyed during special occasions.

SISIG

Sisig is a famous Kapampangan dish made from chopped, grilled pork, usually seasoned with onions, chili, and calamansi, known for its savory, tangy, and slightly crispy texture.

LECHON BABOY

Lechon Baboy is a beloved Kapampangan dish featuring a whole roasted pig with crispy skin and juicy, flavorful meat, typically served at festive celebrations and special gatherings.

PINDANG DAMULAG

Pindang Damulag is a traditional Kapampangan dish made from cured and fermented carabao (water buffalo) meat, known for its distinctively tangy and savory flavor, often served with rice and dipping sauces.

MENUDO

Menudo is a Kapampangan stew made with diced pork, liver, potatoes, and carrots, simmered in a rich tomato-based sauce, known for its hearty and flavorful taste.

KILAYIN

Kilayin is a traditional Kapampangan dish made from pork or offal, marinated in vinegar and spices, then saut√©ed with liver and often seasoned with garlic and chilies, offering a tangy and savory flavor.

DESSERT

BIBINGKA

A Kapampangan rice cake traditionally baked in banana leaves, topped with salted egg and cheese. It has a soft, fluffy texture and is often enjoyed during the holiday season.

BIKO

A sticky rice cake made with glutinous rice, coconut milk, and brown sugar, topped with latik (toasted coconut curds). It has a rich, sweet flavor and is often served during special occasions.

MACAPUNO TARTS

Sweet tarts filled with preserved macapuno (soft coconut meat) and baked in a buttery crust. These bite-sized treats offer a perfect balance of sweetness and texture.

PUTO BUMBONG

A purple rice delicacy steamed in bamboo tubes and served with butter, coconut, and muscovado sugar. It is a popular Christmas treat with a unique chewy texture.

TIBOK-TIBOK

A Kapampangan pudding made from carabao's milk, thickened with cornstarch and flavored with dayap zest. It is smooth, creamy, and usually topped with latik for added flavor.

UBE HALAYA

A sweet, creamy jam made from purple yam, often used as a filling or topping for various desserts. Its vibrant color and rich, earthy flavor make it a Filipino favorite.
